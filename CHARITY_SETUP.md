## Executive Summary

This guide outlines how to establish a registered charity in England & Wales focused on wildlife conservation, with a young person (under 18) in a leadership role. While UK charity law has age restrictions for trustees, there are clear pathways for young founders to lead meaningfully.

---

## Key Legal Considerations for Young Founders

### Age Restrictions in UK Charity Law

| Role | Minimum Age | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Charity Trustee** | 16 years | As of Charities Act 2022 (previously 18) |
| **Company Director** | 16 years | For CIOs and charitable companies |
| **Signing Legal Documents** | 18 years | Contracts, bank accounts, etc. |

### ‚úÖ Good News for Rhea!
As of the **Charities Act 2022**, the minimum age to be a charity trustee in England & Wales was **lowered from 18 to 16**. This means Rhea can legally serve as a trustee!

However, there are practical considerations:
- Some banks require account signatories to be 18+
- Some grant-makers prefer trustees over 18
- An adult co-trustee is recommended for legal/financial matters

## Recommended Structure

### Option 1: Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO) ‚≠ê RECOMMENDED

A CIO is the simplest structure for a new charity:
- ‚úÖ Legal entity (can own assets, enter contracts)
- ‚úÖ Limited liability for trustees
- ‚úÖ Only registers with Charity Commission (not Companies House)
- ‚úÖ Lower admin burden than charitable company

## Step-by-Step Setup Process

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

#### Week 1: Define the Charity

**1. Choose a Name**
Suggestions:
- Endura Conservation Foundation
- Wildlife Guardians UK
- Species Savers
- Rhea's Ark Conservation
- Generation Wild

**2. Define Charitable Objects (Purpose)**

Example charitable objects for registration:
> "The advancement of environmental protection and improvement, in particular the conservation of endangered species worldwide, through:
> - Educational programs raising awareness about endangered species
> - Supporting conservation projects and wildlife sanctuaries
> - Developing technology solutions that engage young people in conservation
> - Funding research into species protection"

**3. Identify Trustees**

Minimum: 3 trustees (recommend 5)
- At least 2-3 adults (18+) for practical/legal matters
- Include Rhea as founding trustee
- Consider: parents, teachers, conservation professionals

#### Week 2: Draft Constitution

The Charity Commission provides a model CIO constitution. Key sections:
1. Name and purposes
2. Powers (what the charity can do)
3. Membership
4. Trustee provisions
5. Decision-making processes
6. Dissolution clause

**Template**: [Charity Commission Model Constitution](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/setting-up-a-charity-model-governing-documents)

#### Week 3: Trustee Recruitment

**Ideal Trustee Profile:**
- Mix of skills (finance, legal, conservation, marketing)
- Passion for wildlife/environment
- Supportive of youth leadership
- Available for quarterly meetings

**Where to Find Trustees:**
- Family/family friends
- School teachers/governors
- Local wildlife trust contacts
- Conservation professionals
- Getting on Board (trustee matching service)

#### Week 4: First Trustee Meeting

**Agenda:**
1. Agree charity name and purposes
2. Approve constitution
3. Appoint Chair, Treasurer, Secretary
4. Agree initial activities
5. Plan registration

---

### Phase 2: Registration (Weeks 5-8)

#### Register with Charity Commission

**Requirements:**
- Completed CIO registration form
- Governing document (constitution)
- Details of all trustees
- Charitable purposes
- Estimated annual income

**When to Register:**
- **Must register** if annual income exceeds ¬£5,000
- **Can register** voluntarily below ¬£5,000 (recommended for credibility)

**How to Register:**
1. Go to: https://www.gov.uk/setting-up-charity
2. Create Charity Commission online account
3. Complete application form
4. Upload constitution
5. Pay ¬£0 (registration is free!)
6. Wait 5-40 days for decision

#### Set Up Bank Account

**Charity Bank Accounts (recommend):**
- **CAF Bank** - Specialist charity bank
- **Unity Trust Bank** - Ethical banking for charities
- **Lloyds/Barclays** - Charity accounts available

**Requirements:**
- Charity registration number (or proof of application)
- ID for all signatories
- Two signatories for payments (usually adults)
- Constitution copy

---

### Phase 3: Operations (Weeks 9-12)

#### Essential Policies

Create these key documents:
1. **Safeguarding Policy** (required when working with young people)
2. **Financial Procedures**
3. **Conflict of Interest Policy**
4. **Data Protection/GDPR Policy**
5. **Volunteer Policy**

#### Set Up Systems

- [ ] Charity email (e.g., info@enduraconservation.org)
- [ ] Simple website
- [ ] Social media accounts
- [ ] Accounting software (Wave, Xero nonprofit)
- [ ] Donor management (optional: Donorfy, Beacon)

## Fundraising in the UK

### Registration Requirements

| Fundraising Type | Registration Needed |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Donations via website | None for small charities |
| Crowdfunding | Platform handles compliance |
| Street collections | Local council license |
| House-to-house | Local council license |
| Charity events | Usually none |
| Grants | Charity registration often required |

### Fundraising Ideas for a Youth-Led Charity

1. **Endura App Integration**
   - % of premium subscriptions to charity
   - "Adopt an animal" in-app purchases
   - Corporate sponsorship of endangered animals

2. **School Partnerships**
   - Assembly presentations
   - Sponsored study-a-thons
   - Inter-school competition leagues

3. **Events**
   - Wildlife awareness walks
   - Virtual study marathons
   - Art competitions

4. **Grants (once registered)**
   - National Lottery Community Fund
   - Local community foundations
   - Environmental trusts
   - Youth-focused funders (e.g., Young Start)
